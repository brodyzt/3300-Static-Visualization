Female Representation in Movies

Inherent issues in society are often mirrored in said society's contemporary media. Due to this fact, taking a more detailed and in depth look into media can illustrate these prevalent patterns. In this data visualization, we seek to illustrate the differences in various factors between movies that pass and fail the Bechdel Test, a test that evaluates whether or not the movie in question contains an instance of two women talking about something other than men, viewing the data at different angles to reveal patterns and trends that illuminate how American society viewed women in the past decades.

Failure Type By Year

The Bechdel test has multiple categories of movies that can fail. This graph shoes the trend in each of these categories over time. The categories of failure are as follows. "No Women" means there were no significant female roles. "No W2W" means there wasn't any communication between two female characters. "Only Talk About Men" means female characters talked to each other, but only about the subject of men. "Disagreement" means that the reviewers for these movies didn't agree 100% on the movies in that category. As seen by the total height of the bars, the overall trend is movies are failing less frequently as time goes on. Another notable trend is that there are substantially less No W2W failures now than in 1970.

Avg. Rating vs. Year

This graph illustrates the progression of movies that pass and fail the Bechdel Test over the last thirty-five years in relation to their average IMDB rating. While there is some variation year to year, it appears that generally, movies that passes the Bechdel test in the 1980s rated significantly lower than those that failed. The average ratings of the movies that passed and those that failed, however, has almost converged within the last 20 years. There is still a small discrepancy, but not nearly as much as 30 years ago.
